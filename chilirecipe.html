Chili

1 hour, serves:
6

Ingredients

2 tablespoons olive oil

1 medium red onion, chopped

1 large red bell pepper, chopped

2 medium carrot, chopped

2 ribs celery, chopped

4 cloves garlic, pressed or minced

2 tablespoons chili powder

2 teaspoons ground cumin

1.5 teaspoons smoked paprika

2 400g can of diced tomatoes with their juices

2 400g can black beans, rinsed and drained

1 400g can pinto beans, rinsed and drained

5 dl vegetable broth or water

1 bay leaf

2 tablespoons chopped fresh cilantro

1 teaspoon sherry vinegar or red wine vinegar or lime juice

Steps

1. In a large Dutch oven or heavy-bottomed pot over medium heat, warm the olive oil until shimmering. Add the chopped onion, bell pepper, carrot, celery and a little salt. Stir to combine and then cook, stirring occasionally, until the vegetables are tender and the onion is translucent, about 7 to 10 minutes.

2. Add the garlic, chili powder, cumin, smoked paprika (go easy on the paprika if you’re sensitive to spice) and oregano. Cook until fragrant while stirring constantly, about 1 minute.

3. Add the diced tomatoes and their juices, the drained black beans and pinto beans, vegetable broth and bay leaf. Stir to combine and let the mixture come to a simmer. Continue cooking, stirring occasionally and reducing heat as necessary to maintain a gentle simmer, for 30 minutes. Remove the chili from heat.

4. Add the chopped cilantro, stir to blend, and then mix in the vinegar, to taste. Add salt to taste too.
